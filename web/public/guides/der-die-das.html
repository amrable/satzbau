How to know if a noun is der, die, or das

Rules for predicting German noun gender — and what to do when the rules don't apply.

Every German noun has a grammatical gender: masculine (der), feminine (die), or neuter (das). Gender controls articles, adjective endings, and pronouns, so guessing wrong changes a lot in one sentence. The honest answer up front: there is no single rule that covers every noun. But about 80% of nouns follow predictable patterns — learn those patterns and you'll guess correctly far more often than by chance.

Rule 1: by noun ending

Endings are the most reliable shortcut. These are near-universal:

EndingUsuallyExamples
-ung, -heit, -keit, -schaft, -ion, -tät, -ik, -urdie (feminine)die Zeitung, die Freiheit, die Universität
-e (most)die (feminine)die Lampe, die Blume, die Tasche
-chen, -leindas (neuter)das Mädchen, das Büchlein
-ment, -tum, -um, -madas (neuter)das Argument, das Datum, das Thema
-er (agent), -ling, -ich, -ig, -ismusder (masculine)der Lehrer, der Frühling, der Tourismus
-or, -ist, -ant, -entder (masculine)der Doktor, der Student, der Tourist

Rule 2: by meaning

Masculine (der)

  • Male people and animals: der Mann, der Vater, der Hund.
  • Days, months, seasons: der Montag, der Juli, der Winter.
  • Weather, directions, points of the compass: der Regen, der Norden.
  • Alcoholic drinks (with one famous exception, das Bier): der Wein.
  • Car brands: der BMW, der Audi.

Feminine (die)

  • Female people and animals: die Frau, die Mutter, die Katze.
  • Most numbers and ordinals as nouns: die Eins.
  • Most trees and flowers: die Eiche, die Rose.
  • Motorcycle and ship brands: die Harley, die Titanic.

Neuter (das)

  • Young creatures: das Kind, das Baby, das Kalb. (Yes, this is why das Mädchen is neuter.)
  • Metals and chemical elements: das Gold, das Eisen.
  • Infinitives used as nouns: das Schwimmen, das Lesen.
  • Most colors and languages as nouns: das Rot, das Deutsch.

Rule 3: compound nouns take the last noun's gender

Compounds are easy: the final noun decides the article. die Tür + der Schlüssel = der Türschlüssel. If you know the simple noun, you know the compound.

What to do when you can't guess

When the ending doesn't help and the meaning doesn't fit any category, there's no shortcut — you have to learn the article with the noun. A few habits that work:

  • Always write new vocabulary as article + noun + plural: die Tür, -en, not just Tür.
  • Read the article out loud every time. After a few weeks the wrong article starts sounding wrong.
  • When you read a new sentence and aren't sure about a word's gender, check it — don't let a guess become a habit.
