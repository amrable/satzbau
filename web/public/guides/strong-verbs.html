German strong & irregular verbs

The most common irregular verbs, grouped by vowel pattern.

German strong verbs change their stem vowel in the past tense and often in the du/er/sie/es present form too. There's no rule that tells you which vowel change applies — you memorize them. The good news: there are groups, and about 200 strong verbs cover almost everything you'll encounter. Below are the most frequent ones.

Vowel-change patterns

Many strong verbs fall into predictable groups. Some of the bigger ones:

  • ei → ie → ie: bleiben–blieb–geblieben, schreiben–schrieb–geschrieben, steigen–stieg–gestiegen.
  • ie → o → o: fliegen–flog–geflogen, verlieren–verlor–verloren, schließen–schloss–geschlossen.
  • i → a → u: singen–sang–gesungen, trinken–trank–getrunken, finden–fand–gefunden.
  • e → a → o: sprechen–sprach–gesprochen, helfen–half–geholfen, nehmen–nahm–genommen.
  • e → a → e: essen–aß–gegessen, geben–gab–gegeben, lesen–las–gelesen.
